How the partnership works
The RMS × TD Africa model
RMS is the sales engine. TD Africa is the fulfilment engine. Every tech deal has a clear division of responsibility — and both parties earn from every transaction.

Who is TD Africa?

TD Africa (Technology Distributions Limited) is Africa's leading technology distributor, founded in Lagos in 1999. They are the authorised distributor for HP, Microsoft, Dell, Cisco, Apple, Lenovo, Samsung, APC (Schneider Electric), IBM, D-Link, Huawei, Bosch and over 20 other global brands. They have 350+ employees, offices in 8 African countries, and a network of over 1,500 resellers. They handle product supply, warehousing, delivery, deployment, installation, and after-sales support. RMS's strategic partnership with TD Africa gives us access to this entire ecosystem — without owning a single product or employing a single engineer.

Why this model is superior to hiring a tech team

Zero capital risk

No inventory, no warehousing, no equipment to depreciate. RMS never holds stock or takes on hardware debt. Every deal is asset-light and fully variable cost.

Zero technical headcount

No engineers, no IT support staff, no Microsoft certifications required. TD Africa's 350+ employees and 25 years of deployment experience are RMS's team by proxy.

Instant product range

TD Africa's portfolio covers every tech need a client could have — 20+ global brands, Microsoft licensing, cloud services, hardware, networking, power solutions, and gadgets. RMS sells all of it from day one.

TD Africa's credibility

When RMS brings TD Africa into a deal, the client gets Africa's most trusted tech distributor — 25-year track record, manufacturer-authorised, HP Distributor of the Year award winner.

Focus stays on research

RMS's competitive advantage is insight, relationships, and data. The tech division adds revenue without diluting that focus — it stays in its lane while TD Africa stays in theirs.

West Africa reach built in

TD Africa operates in 8 African countries. RMS can sell tech to clients in Ghana, Cameroon, Kenya, and more — and TD Africa fulfils it locally. Instant regional scale.

How RMS earns — commission model
Revenue & commission structure
RMS earns in two ways — a commission from TD Africa on every sale referred, and a markup on Microsoft licensing where RMS invoices the client directly at a margin. All revenue is recognised when TD Africa confirms delivery/activation.

Two earning modes

Mode A — Referral commission

RMS refers the client to TD Africa. TD Africa invoices the client directly. TD Africa pays RMS a pre-agreed commission (8–15%) on the invoice value within 30 days of client payment. Used for hardware, networking, and services.

Mode B — Reseller margin

RMS invoices the client directly at the retail price, buys from TD Africa at the reseller price, keeps the margin. Best used for Microsoft licensing (recurring monthly). RMS owns the client billing relationship.

How we sell
Sales pipeline — from spot to close
The sales process is simple. RMS identifies the need. TD Africa fulfils it. Here is every stage with clear ownership.
1
Spot the opportunity RMS
During a research project debrief, SME consultation, or prospect call — the client mentions needing laptops, IT setup, Microsoft licences, networking, or gadgets. The RMS account manager flags this immediately. Key trigger questions: "What laptops does your team use?" / "How do you manage your Microsoft licences?" / "Do you have reliable internet at your field offices?"
2
Qualify the need RMS
RMS asks 4 questions: (1) What is needed? (2) How many units / seats? (3) What is the budget range? (4) What is the timeline? This takes 10 minutes in a meeting. RMS does NOT attempt to spec or recommend — that is TD Africa's job. Just capture the requirement.
3
Submit to TD Africa for quote RMS + TD Africa
RMS sends the requirement to the dedicated TD Africa account contact via WhatsApp/email using a standard RMS referral form. TD Africa provides a quote within 24–48 hours. RMS reviews, adds their commission margin, and presents to client as an "RMS Tech quotation."
4
Present quote to client RMS
RMS presents the quotation on RMS letterhead. The client relationship remains with RMS throughout — TD Africa is the "fulfilment partner" mentioned if asked, but RMS owns the conversation. Present total value: hardware quality + TD Africa's warranty + RMS's ongoing relationship.
5
Client pays, TD Africa delivers RMS + TD Africa
Client pays RMS (Mode B — reseller) OR TD Africa directly (Mode A — referral). TD Africa handles logistics, delivery, installation, and activation. For Microsoft licences, TD Africa provisions the tenant and RMS receives monthly billing reports.
6
RMS collects commission RMS
TD Africa pays RMS commission within 30 days of client payment (referral model) or RMS retains margin at invoicing (reseller model). RMS Finance logs the transaction, reconciles against TD Africa's monthly statement, and records it in the accounts.
7
Upsell & renew RMS
Microsoft licences auto-renew monthly — RMS earns every month with no additional sales effort. Hardware upsell cycles at 2–3 years. RMS account managers schedule quarterly check-ins with tech clients to identify the next need: "How are the laptops working? Has the team grown? Do you need Copilot yet?"

Formalising the partnership
Partnership agreement with TD Africa
The partnership needs to be documented before any deals are referred. Here are the terms to negotiate and the steps to formalise the arrangement.

TD Africa contact for partnership formalisation

TD Africa's business development contact: tdmarketing@tdafrica.com or call +234 902 917 4191. Alternatively, visit tdafrica.com/become-a-partner to initiate the reseller/partner registration process. Ask specifically for the Channel Partner Programme and confirm they have an established commission structure for referral partners.

Key terms to negotiate in the partnership agreement

  • 1
    Commission rates by product category Negotiate first
    Get specific percentage agreements in writing for each category — Microsoft licences, hardware (by brand), networking, power, gadgets. Target: 10–15% on Microsoft, 8–12% on hardware, 8–12% on networking. These rates are negotiable based on volume commitment.
  • 2
    Commission payment terms Negotiate first
    Request: commission paid within 30 days of confirmed client payment. Monthly statement from TD Africa listing all RMS-referred deals and amounts due. Direct bank transfer to RMS account. This is standard for their reseller network.
  • 3
    Client ownership clause Negotiate first
    Critical: the agreement must state that any client referred by RMS remains RMS's client for future tech needs — TD Africa cannot directly solicit or approach RMS-referred clients for new business without RMS. Protects your relationship and recurring commission stream.
  • Pricing transparency Week 1
    Request access to TD Africa's reseller price list for all key SKUs — HP, Dell, Lenovo, Cisco, Microsoft. This allows RMS to build quotations for clients and know the margin before presenting. Usually shared under NDA with registered partners.
  • Dedicated account contact at TD Africa Week 1
    Request a named account manager at TD Africa who handles all RMS referrals — responds to quote requests within 24 hours, provides delivery updates, and escalates issues. Without a dedicated contact, turnaround times will be inconsistent.
  • Volume targets and tier bonuses Week 2
    Negotiate a tiered commission structure: once RMS refers ₦50m+ in annual business, commission rates step up by 2–3%. This incentivises both parties to grow the channel and gives RMS upside as the relationship matures.
  • Warranty and after-sales service standards Week 2
    Confirm TD Africa's warranty commitment to RMS-referred clients: minimum 1-year warranty on all hardware, response time for repairs via TDPlus (their service arm), replacement policy for DOA (dead on arrival) devices. These become RMS's service promises to clients.
  • Co-marketing opportunities Month 1–2
    Explore: joint Insight Breakfast events (RMS research + TD Africa tech showcase), co-branded SME tech + research packages, joint LinkedIn content, and RMS listing as an authorised TD Africa channel partner on their website.
