    FIRS TIN Registration Week 1
    Tax Identification Number from Federal Inland Revenue Service — required for invoicing and contracts. Visit firs.gov.ng or nearest FIRS office with CAC documents.
  • !
    LIRS Business Registration Week 1
    Lagos Internal Revenue Service — register for business premises levy and withholding tax. Required for Lagos-based client contracts.
  • VAT Registration (FIRS) Week 2
    Register for VAT collection and remittance. Required to issue VAT invoices to corporate clients. 7.5% standard rate applies.
  • NSITF Registration Week 2
    Nigeria Social Insurance Trust Fund — mandatory for all employers. 1% of employee monthly payroll.
  • ITF Registration Week 2
    Industrial Training Fund — 1% of annual payroll for organisations with 5+ staff or ₦50m+ turnover.
  • NHF Registration Month 1
    National Housing Fund — register with Federal Mortgage Bank. Deduct 2.5% of basic salary from each employee.
  • Pension Fund Administrator (PenCom) Month 1
    Select a licensed PFA. Employer contributes 10%, employee 8% of monthly emolument. Mandatory once headcount ≥ 3.
  • NiMRA Membership Month 1
    Nigeria Marketing Research Association — professional credibility, directory listing, industry access. Contact nimra.ng. Annual fee applies.
  • Professional Indemnity Insurance Month 1
    PI cover protects against claims from errors in research outputs. Minimum ₦50m cover recommended. Contact AXA Mansard, Leadway, or NEM Insurance.
  • Cyber Liability Insurance Month 2
    Covers data breaches and PII exposure. Critical given respondent panel and client data handling.
  • NDPR Compliance Framework Month 1
    Nigeria Data Protection Regulation — data audit, privacy policy, consent management, breach response plan. File compliance audit with NITDA annually.

SME Market Opportunity
Nigeria's 39.6 million MSMEs are an almost entirely untapped research market. This section defines the opportunity, the products, the channels, and the ₦25m+ revenue it adds to our 2026 plan.

Why SMEs? The opportunity CMRG and others have largely ignored.

Nigeria's MSME sector contributes approximately 50% of GDP and accounts for 86.3% of employment (NBS 2021). Yet fewer than 3% of SMEs have ever commissioned formal market research. The reason is simple: the cost model of traditional agencies makes research inaccessible to businesses below ₦500m in annual revenue. RMS is purpose-built to close this gap with standardised, affordable, fast products that deliver professional-grade insight at a price SMEs can justify.

Key Financial Policies

Payment Terms (all clients)

Corporate projects: 40% mobilisation on PO, 40% on fieldwork completion, 20% on final report delivery. NGO/Govt: 50%/50% or project-milestone aligned. SME: 50% upfront, 50% on delivery. Retainers: monthly in advance. No final report released until 100% payment received.

Cash & DSO Targets

Days Sales Outstanding targets: Q1 ≤ 60 days, Q2 ≤ 45 days, Q3 ≤ 38 days, Q4 ≤ 35 days. Monthly AR aging review by Finance. Escalation to CEO after 60 days overdue. Maintain minimum 8-week payroll cash reserve at all times.

Pricing Floor Rules

No project may be accepted below 35% gross margin (post-direct costs). BizDev Lead must get Research Lead sign-off on any scope that risks margin. SME products are priced to deliver ≥55% margin (low delivery cost). No discounts beyond 10% without CEO approval.

VAT & Tax Compliance

All invoices to corporate and government clients include 7.5% VAT. Monthly VAT returns filed with FIRS by the 21st of each following month. WHT (typically 5% on professional fees) deducted by corporate clients — ensure certificates are collected for annual tax reconciliation. Quarterly CIT installments filed.
