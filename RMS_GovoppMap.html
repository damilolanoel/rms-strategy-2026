How to Enter Government Contracts — Practical Playbook
Step 1 — Registration
Get on vendor lists first
Register RMS with the Lagos State Public Procurement Agency (PPA) and Ekiti State Bureau of Public Procurement (EKBPP). Submit CAC certificate, TIN, professional profile, and company profile. This is the gateway — without it, you cannot be awarded any government contract. Takes 2–4 weeks.
Step 2 — NiMRA Membership
Professional credibility for research bids
NiMRA (Nigeria Marketing Research Association) membership is the professional credential government agencies look for when evaluating research proposals. Contact nimra.ng. Annual fee applies. Also register with APCON (Advertising Practitioners Council of Nigeria) for communications research work.
Step 3 — Relationship building
Identify the procurement officer in each MDA
Every MDA has a Director of Finance & Administration and a Procurement Officer. These are your entry contacts, not the Commissioner. Send a formal company introduction letter to each target MDA — physical letter, not email. Follow up with a visit. This is how government vendor relationships are built in Nigeria.
Step 4 — Expression of Interest (EOI)
Submit EOIs proactively, don't wait for RFPs
Most government research work is awarded through EOIs and direct engagements, not open tenders. Submit unsolicited proposals to MDAs with clear research needs (LSETF, LASG Health, Ekiti Ministry of Education). Frame it as "we propose to support your 2026 programme evaluation at no upfront cost — pay only on delivery."
Step 5 — For tech contracts (Lagos)
Route through TD Africa's government channel
TD Africa already has existing relationships with Lagos State MDAs as an authorised distributor. Work with your TD Africa account contact to identify which MDAs are in active procurement cycles for IT equipment. They can name you as the referring partner in their government bids, and you earn the referral commission.
Step 6 — For tech contracts (Ekiti)
Engage the Bureau of ICT directly
Ekiti's Bureau of ICT regulates ALL hardware and software acquisition across MDAs. A single relationship with the Director of the Bureau gives RMS visibility across every Ekiti agency needing computers, networking, and Microsoft licensing. Reach out to: Bureau of ICT, Ekiti State Secretariat, Ado-Ekiti. Reference the EDTP (Ekiti Digital Transformation Programme).

Important compliance notes
  • All contracts above ₦50m require Public Procurement Agency approval
  • Research firms need evidence of past government work or academic partnerships
  • For donor-funded govt programmes (World Bank, AfDB), procurement follows donor guidelines — not Nigerian procurement law
  • Payment terms for government: typically 60–90 days post-invoice with ITAS clearance
  • Budget cycle: submit EOIs Oct–Dec for next year's budget allocation
