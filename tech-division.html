Why RMS is adding a Tech Division
Our existing research clients — and the 39.6m SMEs we serve — all have the same unmet need: working technology. We are already inside their organisations. Adding Microsoft licensing and B2B tech services turns us from a quarterly engagement into a monthly recurring relationship.

The CEO's strategic logic in one paragraph

Every FMCG brand, fintech, SME, and NGO that commissions research from us is simultaneously paying someone else for Microsoft licences, IT support, and cloud services. That money is leaving the room. By becoming a Microsoft CSP Indirect Reseller and adding a lean B2B tech services team, RMS captures recurring monthly revenue from the same clients we already trust us — without acquiring new relationships from scratch. The licensing arm runs on thin margins but builds permanent monthly cash flow. The services arm (cloud migration, IT support, Microsoft 365 deployment) runs on 60–70% margins with minimal field cost. Together they add ₦42m to our 2026 plan and push total revenue to ₦219m.

Division 1 — Microsoft CSP
Microsoft Licence Provisioning
RMS becomes a Microsoft CSP Indirect Reseller — buying licences from a distributor and reselling to Nigerian and African companies and SMEs, managing billing, provisioning, and first-line support.

What is Microsoft CSP?

The Cloud Solution Provider (CSP) programme is Microsoft's official channel for reselling Microsoft 365, Azure, Dynamics 365, and other cloud products. As an Indirect Reseller, RMS signs up under a distributor (Westcon-Comstor Africa or Ingram Micro) who handles the direct Microsoft relationship and billing. RMS then sells, provisions, and supports licences to our clients, earning a reseller margin on every seat per month. Being in Nigeria's Africa CSP region means we can sell to clients across all 18 African countries in that region.

Step-by-step CSP registration
How to become a Microsoft CSP Indirect Reseller
This is the exact sequence to follow. Total time from start to first licence sold: approximately 3–10 business days. All steps are online.
  • 1
    Create a Microsoft work account (M365 tenant) Day 1
    Go to microsoft.com/en-ng/microsoft-365 and sign up for a Microsoft 365 Business trial. Use your business email domain (@reliablemarketsurvey.com). This creates your "work account" (Azure AD tenant) needed for Partner Center. Note: you must have set up your company domain first — this ties to your professional email you already set up on Google Workspace OR create a separate Microsoft 365 tenant for the company.
  • 2
    Join the Microsoft AI Cloud Partner Programme (MAICPP) Day 1
    Go to: partner.microsoft.com → click "Join now" → sign in with your work account. Complete the registration form with your CAC-registered company details (legal name, address, phone). A Microsoft Partner Network (MPN) ID will be issued within minutes. Save this ID — it is required for every subsequent step. Free to join.
  • 3
    Enroll as CSP Indirect Reseller in Partner Center Day 1–2
    Go to: partnercenter.microsoft.com → Account settings → Enroll → Select "Cloud Solution Provider" → Select "Indirect Reseller." Fill in company legal details, support contact information (email + phone RMS will use for client support), and accept the Microsoft Partner Agreement (MPA). Microsoft will verify your account — this typically takes 3–5 business days. You'll receive an email to macount@microsoft.com confirming verification status.
  • Choose and connect with a Distributor (Indirect Provider) Week 1–2
    You MUST connect with an approved Microsoft distributor in the Africa region before you can transact. Two recommended options for Nigeria:
    Option A — Westcon-Comstor Africa: westconcomstor.com — Africa's primary Microsoft distributor, strong Nigeria presence. Contact: africa.microsoft@westcon.com
    Option B — Ingram Micro Africa: ingrammicro.com — global leader, automated cloud marketplace. Contact their Africa team.
    Option C — Dynamics Africa (4Sight): dynamicsafrica.biz — specialist Microsoft CSP provider in Africa. Email: csp@dynamicsafrica.biz
    The distributor handles billing with Microsoft; RMS owns the customer relationship and charges clients directly.
  • Accept Distributor Partnership Invitation Week 2
    Once you contact a distributor and they agree to onboard you, they will send a 1-click partnership invitation link via email to your Partner Center Global Admin account. Accept it in Partner Center. Once accepted, your distributor's name will appear under your "Indirect providers" list. You can now place orders through their portal on behalf of your clients.
  • Set up Partner Center billing and payment Week 2
    Add your business bank account (or credit card) for your distributor's billing. Most Africa distributors invoice monthly in USD or local currency equivalent. Your company will be billed by the distributor; you then invoice your clients in Naira at your marked-up rate. Set up a dedicated USD account (which you already have as a domiciliary account) to handle FX settlement with distributors.
  • Complete Microsoft partner verification (identity + business) Week 2
    Microsoft will conduct business verification including: company name vs CAC records, email domain ownership, employment/identity verification of primary contact (CEO). Keep your CAC certificate, utility bill (office address), and BVN ready. Verification status appears in Partner Center under Account Settings → Legal Info. If not verified within 5 business days, contact Partner Center support.
  • Sign the Microsoft Partner Agreement (MPA) Week 2
    A digital agreement in Partner Center. The Global Admin of your Microsoft tenant must review and accept it. This is a standard digital signature — no physical document required. Required before you can transact any CSP subscription. Sign it during your Partner Center enrollment steps.
  • Provision your first test client (internal) Week 2–3
    Before selling to external clients, provision Microsoft 365 Business Basic for RMS itself through Partner Center. This validates your distributor relationship, confirms billing flows, and gives your team hands-on experience with the provisioning process. Walk through: creating a customer tenant → assigning licences → confirming the client receives their login credentials.
  • Build your client onboarding kit Month 1
    Create the documents your clients will need: (1) Microsoft Customer Agreement (MCA) — the client must sign this before RMS can provision licences on their behalf. (2) RMS Licence Order Form — captures client company name, tenant domain, number of seats, product SKU, billing preference. (3) Provisioning SOP — step-by-step guide for your Tech team to follow each time. (4) Monthly invoice template for client billing in Naira.
  • Join Microsoft SMB & CSP training programmes Month 1–2
    Enroll in free Microsoft Partner training via Microsoft Learn (learn.microsoft.com) and Partner Center. Recommended courses: Microsoft 365 Sales & Technical Fundamentals, Azure Sales Fundamentals, Security Fundamentals. Completing these earns "Solution Partner" designations which increase your credibility, margin rates, and access to Microsoft co-sell opportunities.
  • Set Naira pricing and FX policy Month 1
    Microsoft licences are priced in USD. You will bill clients in Naira. Policy: Use CBN official rate + 3–5% FX buffer at time of invoice, locked for the billing month. Review and reset monthly. Add a clause to client agreements stating that pricing is subject to FX adjustment with 30 days notice. This protects RMS from Naira devaluation risk.
Division 2 — B2B Tech Services
B2B Technology Services
Beyond licensing, RMS Tech offers a suite of managed technology services to companies and SMEs — covering cloud migration, IT support, Microsoft 365 deployment, cybersecurity, and digital transformation advisory.

Why B2B tech services — not just licensing

Licence provisioning has thin margins (~15–25%). B2B tech services — deployment, migration, support retainers, training — carry 60–70% margins and create deep client stickiness. A client who has RMS managing their IT environment does not leave. They also become significantly more likely to commission research (they've already decided to trust us). The services arm converts our CSP licensing clients into long-term managed service relationships, and converts our research clients into IT clients. This is the stickiest revenue model available to an SME-focused professional services firm.

Service Lines

Cloud Migration Services

Move client infrastructure, email, files, and applications to Microsoft Azure or Microsoft 365 cloud. Scoped, phased migrations with minimal downtime.

Typical scope: on-prem → M365, legacy email → Exchange Online, file servers → SharePoint/OneDrive, on-prem SQL → Azure SQL

Microsoft 365 Deployment & Setup

Full deployment, configuration, and staff training for new Microsoft 365 tenants. Includes email migration, Teams setup, SharePoint intranet, OneDrive rollout.

Covers 5-seat SMEs to 200-seat corporates. Training workshops included.

Managed IT Support Retainers

Monthly retainer covering first-line and second-line IT support for Microsoft environments. Remote helpdesk, user account management, licence renewals, patch management.

Tiered plans from ₦150k/month (up to 10 users) to ₦800k/month (up to 100 users)

Cybersecurity & Compliance

Deploy Microsoft Defender, Intune, Conditional Access, and Multi-Factor Authentication. NDPR compliance gap assessments and remediation. Email security (anti-phishing, spam filtering).

Critical upsell for financial services and healthcare clients

Microsoft Copilot & AI Enablement

Provision and deploy Microsoft 365 Copilot. Workshops on prompt engineering, Copilot for Teams, Word, Excel, and Outlook. Custom Copilot agents via Copilot Studio.

Fast-growing segment — Nigeria Copilot adoption is early-stage, high margin opportunity

Power Platform & Business Apps

Build business apps, automation flows, and dashboards using Power Apps, Power Automate, and Power BI — all within the Microsoft 365 licensing clients already have.

Cross-sell: RMS Power BI research dashboards are a natural entry point here

IT Procurement & Hardware Advisory

Advisory on laptop, server, network, and peripheral procurement for SMEs setting up or upgrading their IT infrastructure. Vendor-neutral recommendations, procurement facilitation.

Commission-based referral model — low risk, moderate revenue

Microsoft Training & Adoption

Staff training programmes on Microsoft 365 tools — Teams, SharePoint, Power BI, Outlook, Word, Excel. In-person or virtual. Tailored to the client's actual environment.

Day rates from ₦250k (virtual, up to 20 users) to ₦500k (in-person, full day)

Why the tech division ramp is back-loaded

Q1 revenue from tech is deliberately low (₦1.5m combined) because CSP registration takes 2–4 weeks and the first licensing clients need to go through provisioning and the Microsoft Customer Agreement before they can be billed. B2B services projects will begin closing in Q2 once the Tech Lead is in place and the first deployment case study is complete. By Q4 the tech division should be running at ₦18m/quarter run rate, which annualises to ₦72m — making it the second-largest division in 2027.
